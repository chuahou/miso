\(\Rightarrow\) CLRS 17.

  • [1.3] Sequence of operations with costs \(c_1,\ldots,c_n\) has amortized cost \(\leq C\) if \(\sum_{k=1}^i c_k \leq Ci\) for all \(i\).
    • Less crude than worst case \(\max c_i\).
  • [1.7] Aggregate method: find aggregate cost \(T(n)\) of \(n\) ops, then \(T(n)/n\).
  • [1.9] Accounting/banker’s method:
    • Assign operation \(i\) charge \(x_i\).
    • Credit banked if \(x_i > c_i\), used if \(x_i < c_i\).
    • Prove credit \(\geq 0\), then \(\sum c \leq \sum x\) and amortized cost \(\leq \max x_i\).
  • [1.10] Potential method:
    • Potential \(\phi : \text{State} \to \mathbb R^+\) (cf. credit).
    • Charge \(x_i = c_i + \phi_i - \phi_{i-1}\).
    • If \(\phi_0 = 0\) then \(\sum c \leq \sum x\) and amortized cost \(\leq \max x_i\).
